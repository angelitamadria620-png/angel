About Agoo

A small historic town in La Union with beautiful beaches and rich culture.

Our History

Agoo, La Union is one of the oldest towns in the province, founded on December 8, 1578 by Spanish conquistador Juan de Salcedo. Originally part of Pangasinan, it became one of the twelve original towns of La Union when the province was created in 1850. Its history is deeply tied to early evangelization, coastal trade, and cultural heritage.

Our Culture

  • Old churches from Spanish era
  • Traditional dances and music
  • Ilocano food
  • Festivals like Dinengdeng and Kapatiran
  • Arts and crafts preserved in Museo de Iloko
  • Symbols such as the Eagle of the North

Vision

To be a good tourism place that keeps our history, celebrates our culture, protects our environment, and helps our people grow together in unity and progress.

Mission

To show our culture through festivals, food, and traditions; protect nature by promoting sustainable tourism; preserve old churches and heritage sites; and support local people by creating opportunities in tourism, education, and livelihood.

What We Value

Helping each other (Bayanihan) Caring for nature Being friendly Our history
